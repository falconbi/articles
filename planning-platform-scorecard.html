Bottom line

TM1 only wins when you deliberately weight for the builder's seat. Under a balanced or user-centric view, Pigment edges ahead — largely because ease of use and adoption keep the work (and the data) inside the governed environment.

The common mistake: buying for the builder's ceiling and then losing everyday users to spreadsheets — or buying for the clean interface and hitting a wall on an ambitious build. The scores don't pick the tool. Whose seat you're optimising for does.

Method & honesty note. Scores (1–10) are a considered calibration, not vendor benchmarks — reasonable people could shift any single score a point or two. The weighted total is the score-weighted average, normalised so the weights always resolve even when they don't sum to 100. The three preset lenses are illustrative; plug in your own organisation's real weights and the totals move. Treat this as a reasoning aid for a decision, not a verdict handed down.
